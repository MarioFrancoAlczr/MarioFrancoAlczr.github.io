  • STEPS TO SIGN A DOCUMENT WITH YOUR DNI

  • 1-PREPARATIONS

    Before starting the installation, I take it for granted that you have the "slip" with the keys that they gave you at the DNIe issuing office and of course you also have to have a card reader installed on your computer.

  • 2-INSTALL SOFTWARE AND DRIVERS

    1-Install Dnie reader: If your operating system is Windows with versions W7, W8, W8.1 and Windows 10, you will only have to insert the DNIe card in the cryptographic reader and the Operating System will install the DNIe driver automatically.
    2-Install the necessary software on your computer to be able to interact properly with the cryptographic cards and with the DNI 3.0. Your computer will need to have installed "pieces" of software called cryptographic modules

  • 3-CHECK THAT THE DNIe IS PROPERTLY INSTALLED

    Once installed, check that you can see the certificates in your computer's browser
    1– Check that the browser detects your electronic ID.

    2-Check in the same window, by clicking on "Trusted Certification Entities" that all the root certificates of the FNMT have been installed.

  • HOW TO SIGN IN A PDF WITH THE ELECTRONICS ID

    1-Open the PDF document to digitally sign it with your dnie.
    2-Click on "tools" at the top and then on the "Certificates" icon

    3-Click on "Digital Signature" in the certificates menu at the top

  • HOW TO SIGN IN A PDF WITH THE ELECTRONICS ID (2)

    4-Drag the box of your signature in the place where you want to sign and then your electronic ID should appear in the list of certificates, select it and click on the button «continue» and «sign». It will ask you for the PIN, you add it and that's it.
    5-Save the document with a new name or replace the current document. Your signature will have been embedded with your electronic ID data, with the current date and time.

  • OTHER THINGS

    If you have had any problems, check that you have the cryptographic reader properly installed and that the private keys of the dnie are not expired, since the validity of the recognized electronic certificates incorporated into the card may not exceed five years, regardless of the validity of the card-holder.
